# LiteRealm Story Bible

## Direction
LiteRealm is a bleak open-world survival shooter about rebuilding a refuge inside a valley that repeats the same disaster every night. The tone should feel tactical, sparse, and dangerous: low trust, scarce supplies, short contracts, harsh travel, and story fragments recovered from bodies, radios, ledgers, and caches.

The goal is not to copy any specific game. The target feel is a hard survival loop: leave shelter, accept a risky contract, move across hostile ground, scavenge, finish objectives, extract alive, level up, and unlock the next tier of danger.

## Premise
The valley was once a private logistics corridor called LiteRealm, used to move medical stockpiles, fuel, and emergency radio gear between mountain settlements. When the outbreak hit, the corridor became a sealed killing floor. Survivors now talk about the "Ash Crown", a signal source beneath the red-pillar clearing that attracts infected, bends radio traffic, and keeps drawing desperate people into the same trap.

Ranger Vale is one of the few people still trying to turn the valley into a refuge. He does not ask for heroics. He offers contracts, pays in supplies, and slowly reveals that the infected are not the only threat. Somebody fed survivor names and route codes to the thing in the clearing.

## Core Story Arc
Act I: The Smoke Line
The player proves they can survive outside camp. They clear Marker 12, recover the Cabin Tape, and find the first route codes.

Act II: The Black Pine Ledger
The player pushes into deeper points of interest, recovers a ledger of betrayed survivors, secures the ruins, and brings the old relay online.

Act III: The Ash Crown
The player uses the relay and lure beacon to wake the source under the clearing, defeats the Crown, and recovers its core.

Epilogue: Dead Air
The player opens the final cache and chooses the implied future of the valley: build a refuge or keep moving.

## Progression Pillars
- Objective XP matters. Field objectives grant XP as soon as they are completed.
- Turn-in XP and supplies still matter. Returning alive is part of progression.
- Story contracts are level-gated, creating a clear campaign spine.
- Quest text should carry the drama, while objectives stay readable and practical.
- Locations should feel like tactical contracts rather than fantasy errands.
